About University of Eastern Pangasinan

📜 Story time

The University of Eastern Pangasinan is a local university in Binalonan, Pangasinan, Philippines, created to give accessible college education to students in the eastern part of the province. It was founded in 2005 through the initiative of local leaders, especially then Binalonan Mayor (now Governor) Ramon “Monmon” Guico III, with the goal of making higher education more available to ordinary families in the area. In its early days, UEP started with very humble beginnings. It was first planned as part of a vision to build a “world-class” but affordable university for students who could not easily go to schools in big cities. The idea was to provide programs that are useful for jobs and community development.

The university began operations in the mid-2000s and grew quickly. It started offering programs like Business Administration, Education, Information Technology, Criminology, Midwifery, and Hospitality Management. Over time, more buildings and facilities were added, including a main tower that can accommodate thousands of students. As it developed, UEP also became active in community service—holding outreach programs, training, and local development projects to help nearby towns. It is known not only as a school but also as a partner in improving education and livelihood in the community. Today, UEP continues to grow as one of the important local universities in Pangasinan, focusing on accessible education, skills training, and community involvement..

Mission

University of Eastern Pangasinan is dedicated to producing highly competent and morally responsible UEPians through innovation, industry-focused education, sustainable research, and values-based curriculum. We commit to shaping ethical trailblazers bringing our principle-centered culture defining our values of Maka-Diyos, Marunong and Malinis.
